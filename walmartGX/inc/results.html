Basic New PPO Plan

This may be a good plan for you if:

  • You are more comfortable paying less from your paycheck and more when you receive care.
  • You might have major surgery, have a baby, or have other major medical services in the coming year.
Remember: Preventive care is covered 100% for all Premier Company medical plans when you use an in-network provider.

Health Care Flexible Spending Account (FSA)

If you enroll in the Basic PPO Plan or the HMO Plan, you can contribute to a Health Care Flexible Spending Account (FSA). With the Health Care FSA, you contribute pre-tax dollars to your account. You can use the money in your account to be reimbursed for eligible medical, dental, and vision expenses. If you know you’ll have out-of-pocket costs for health care during the year, you might want to take advantage of the Health Care FSA.

High Deductible Health Plan (HDHP) with HSA

This may be a good plan for you if:

  • You want to pay less from your paycheck and more when you receive care.
  • You are not planning to need major surgery, have a baby, or have other major medical services.
  • You want to be able to contribute to a Health Savings Account (HSA) to start saving for eligible medical expenses or save it for future medical expenses.
Remember: Preventive care is covered 100% for all Premier Company medical plans when you use an in-network provider.

Health Savings Account (HSA)

If you enroll in the High Deductible Health Plan, you’ll have the opportunity to save pre-tax money in a Health Savings Account (HSA). You can use the funds to pay for a variety of qualified health care expenses. Contributing to your HSA lowers your taxable income, and you are not taxed on what you use. In addition, any interest your HSA earns is not taxed. The money in an HSA can accumulate over time—there is no "use it or lose it rule" and the funds are yours to keep.

Health Care Flexible Spending Account (FSA)

If you enroll in this plan, you are not eligible to contribute to a Health Care Flexible Spending Account (FSA). However, you will be able to use the funds from your HSA to cover eligible medical expenses.
